    Intraday Variation in Cross-sectional Stock Comovement and Impact of Index-based Strategies

    We investigate how comovement of S&P 500 stocks changes during a day using a large highfrequency dataset and estimators that are robust under microstructure noise and asynchronicity. We find that, in the recent decade, the stock correlation increases substantially throughout the trading session, while the cross-sectional beta dispersion decreases. These patterns suggest that S&P 500 stocks exhibit stronger comovement near the market close. We explain the time-varying comovement by the intraday variation in the composition of index-based and firm-based order flows. A cross-section market impact model with time-varying demand from single-stock and index investors generates the observed intraday patterns.

    National Populism --- Roger Eatwell, Matthew Goodwin
    • Myth & Promises:
      • Untrue to simply think that national populism only attracts a particular group of voters
      • Core values:
        1. Popular will
        2. Defend plain, ordinary people
        3. Replace corrupt and distant elites
      • Not only about economics; other important concerns, e.g., immigrants, community destruction
    • Four D’s for national populism:
      1. Distrust: distrust of the ‘governance elites’ that become less representative of voters (transmission of power from national governments to transnational organizations)
      2. Destruction: fears about the destruction of community and, triggered by hyper ethic change, religious and cultural conflict, and security issues.
      3. Deprivation: the aggressive expansion of neoliberalism (globalization, deregulation) increased the wealth inequality and led to feelings of deprivation (objective and subjective) for working class
      4. Dealignment: new cultural conflicts weaken the bonds between voters and traditional parties, this leads to rising volatility and apathetic voters that create room for populism
    • Impact:
      • Mainstream parties increasingly absorb agenda and policy from national populism; causing the emergence of 'national-populist-lite'

    A Better Way to Put Your Data to Work (Harvard Business Review, Jul-Aug 2022)
    • Challenges in using data:
      • Trade-off between near-term value vs. future use
      • Sometimes neither 'monolithic' and 'grassroot' methods don't work
      • Successful strategy: treat data like a product
      • Base prototype + flexibility for future/different use or improvement
      • Balance near-term versus future usage
    • Data product: high-quality, ready-to-use, versatility for applications (like Lego bricks)
    • 'Consumer'-oriented; consumption archetypes:
      • Digital applications
      • Advanced analytics systems
      • Reporting systems
      • Discovery sandboxes
      • External data-sharing
    • Managing data:
      • Product manager + expert
      • Balance between impact/feasibility/speed
